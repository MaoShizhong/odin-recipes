White Cut Chicken


Description

White cut chicken is a simple poached chicken that is deceptively tasty!
The leftover poaching liquid can then be reused as chicken stock and incorporated into many other dishes!

Ingredients:

  • Whole chicken
  • 3 stalks of scallion (a.k.a. spring onion/green onion)
  • Thumb-size piece of ginger (peeled and bruised, or sliced)
  • 1 tbsp salt

Steps:

  1. Boil enough water in a pot large enough to fully submerge a whole chicken (if you do not have a large enough pot, feel free to cut the chicken into whatever size fits).
  2. Add the salt, scallion and ginger to the water and keep at a rolling boil.
  3. Carefully submerge the chicken in the boiling water, dipping in and out to make sure the cavity is filled with boiling water (otherwise it will not cook evenly).
  4. Poach the chicken for at least 45 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked through.
  5. Remove the chicken and plunge into an ice bath to stop the cooking process and keep the chicken smooth - leave in the ice bath for at least 20 minutes.
  6. Remove the chicken, pat dry and chop/shred however you desire! Best served cold with ginger scallion oil!
