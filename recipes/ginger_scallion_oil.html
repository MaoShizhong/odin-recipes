Ginger Scallion Oil

Description:

Ginger scallion oil - one of the simplest but most treasured sauces of Cantonese cuisine!
This sauce goes wonderfully with our white-cut chicken recipe, giving you the perfect blend of light and sweet with sharp and tangy.

Ingredients:

  • Scallions (approx. same amount as ginger when chopped)
  • Ginger (approx. same amount as scallions when chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • Sand ginger powder (optional but highly recommended)
  • Any neutral oil

Steps:

  1. Finely chop the scallions and ginger and mix in a bowl.
  2. If you have or can get sand ginger powder (a relative of ginger/galangal which is not very spicy but much more gentle and aromatic) then add about a teaspoon and mix in well.
  3. Add salt to taste (you ideally want this a little on the salty side as adding the hot oil later will round out some of the saltiness.
  4. Heat some neutral oil (e.g. rapeseed oil) in a pan until very hot (if you dropped a small piece of the ginger/scallion mix in, it would immediately sizzle).
  5. Once hot enough, pour the hot oil over the ginger/scallion mix, then mix well with a spoon.
  6. Allow to cool before tasting and adding more salt if needed. Best served on the colder side which will add to that sharpness and tang!
